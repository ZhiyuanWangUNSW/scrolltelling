Our Position

Every health professional must learn how to provide quality health care to people with intellectual disability because everyone has the right to safe, respectful, effective and inclusive health care.

The Problem

People with intellectual disability experience some of the poorest health outcomes in Australia. Many of these outcomes are preventable with better access to inclusive, high-quality care.

As the document notes, “University courses teach very little, if anything, about intellectual disability…” and most health professionals are not required to complete training in this area.

This gap leaves much of the workforce underprepared to deliver accessible and equitable care.

The Solution

Better education and training is one of the top requests made by people with intellectual disability. The Position Statement calls for a coordinated national approach to upskill today’s and tomorrow’s health workforce.

Training must be grounded in human rights, reflect lived experience, encourage self-reflection, and align with best-practice person-centred care.

As Lucy, a project worker with lived experience, says: “All health workers need to respect people with intellectual disability and treat them with fairness…”

Key Recommendations

  • Make intellectual disability education compulsory in all tertiary health courses.
  • Increase opportunities for clinical and non-clinical placements involving people with intellectual disability.
  • Actively involve people with intellectual disability and their support networks in designing and delivering training.
  • Require intellectual disability training for all health professionals in public hospitals and emergency services.
  • Ensure peak bodies and specialist colleges promote CPD opportunities.
  • Embed intellectual disability content in specialty training curricula.
  • Expand high-quality training and advanced pathways for those who wish to specialise.
  • Provide health professionals with the time, resources and support needed to apply their training.

How This Statement Was Created

The Position Statement draws on best evidence and insights from people with intellectual disability, families, carers, health professionals, educators, researchers and advocates.

Glossary of Key Terms

This section summarises important terms from the document:

  • Clinical placement: Learning in a hospital or clinic.
  • Compulsory: Something that must be done.
  • Person-centred care: Care based on what the person wants and needs.
  • Reasonable adjustments: Changes that remove barriers for people with disability.
  • Support networks: Family, carers and others who help someone.
